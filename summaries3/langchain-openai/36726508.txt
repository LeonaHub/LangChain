The text discusses the relationship between coaches' transformational leadership, athletes' basic psychological needs, and athletes' well-being in competitive sports. It highlights the importance of coaches' leadership in enhancing athletes' well-being and how meeting basic psychological needs can positively impact well-being. The study explores the mediating role of basic psychological needs and the moderating role of gender in this relationship. The results show that coaches' transformational leadership positively influences athletes' well-being, with basic psychological needs mediating this relationship. Additionally, gender moderates the impact, with female athletes experiencing greater improvements in well-being when their basic psychological needs are met compared to male athletes. The study suggests practical implications for coaches to enhance athletes' well-being through transformational leadership and individualized care.
