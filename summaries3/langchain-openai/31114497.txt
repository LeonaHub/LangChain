The text discusses a pharmacovigilance study conducted in the Campania region of Italy to assess the preventability of bleeding cases related to oral anticoagulant therapy reported from 2012 to 2017. The study aimed to evaluate preventability using the P-method, identifying critical criteria related to healthcare professionals' practices. Findings revealed that a significant proportion of bleeding cases were preventable, with drug-drug interactions being a common critical criterion. The study highlighted the importance of improving pharmacovigilance activities to reduce medication errors and inappropriate prescriptions in oral anticoagulant therapy, emphasizing the need for initiatives to enhance drug safety and patient outcomes in the region.
