The text discusses the importance of integrated care for individuals with comorbid behavioral and physical health conditions, particularly those with serious mental illness. It highlights four national integrated care initiatives and their key components, such as multidisciplinary teams, population health management, access to care, decision support, self-management support, care management, referral processes, coordination of care, quality improvement, and sustainability strategies. The text emphasizes the need for comprehensive assessment of program quality and costs, as well as the importance of workforce, health information technology, and addressing social determinants of health in integrated care models.
