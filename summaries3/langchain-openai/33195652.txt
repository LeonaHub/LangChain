The text discusses a rare case of intracranial malignant solitary fibrous tumor (SFT) metastasizing to the chest wall. SFT is a fibroblastic mesenchymal neoplasm that typically occurs in the pleura but can also affect the central nervous system. The case study highlights the challenges in diagnosing and treating SFT, emphasizing the importance of histological and immunohistochemical examinations for accurate diagnosis. The text also mentions the potential for aggressive behavior in a minority of SFT cases, the need for long-term monitoring due to the risk of recurrence and metastasis, and the significance of complete surgical resection for improved outcomes. Additionally, it discusses treatment options such as surgery, radiotherapy, and targeted therapies like pazopanib. The case underscores the importance of thorough examinations and careful follow-up in managing patients with SFT.
