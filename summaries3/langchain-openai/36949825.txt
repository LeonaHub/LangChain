The text discusses post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) in Ethiopia, focusing on a meta-analysis and systematic review conducted to determine the prevalence and associated factors of PTSD among trauma survivors. The study included 13 articles with a total of 5874 participants, revealing a pooled prevalence of PTSD in Ethiopia at 39.28%. Factors associated with PTSD included poor social support, being female, having a previous history of mental illness, and witnessing traumatic events. The study highlighted the importance of social support in preventing PTSD and the need for targeted interventions for those at risk. Limitations included the lack of specificity in age groups and gender prevalence in the primary studies. Immediate interventions are recommended to address the high burden of PTSD in Ethiopia.
