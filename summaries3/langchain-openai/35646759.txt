The text discusses a study that aimed to determine factors influencing COVID-19 preventive behaviors among Thai people using the PRECEDE model. The study analyzed data from a rapid survey conducted online between March and May 2020, with 6,521 participants. Factors such as social support, age, flu-like symptoms, gender, perceived risk of exposure, lockdown policy, and residential area were found to influence COVID-19 preventive behaviors. Social support was the most influential factor. The study highlighted the importance of understanding individual characteristics, predisposing, enabling, and reinforcing factors in promoting COVID-19 preventive behaviors. The results suggested the need for tailored strategies to encourage the adoption of preventive behaviors to reduce the spread of COVID-19 in Thailand.
