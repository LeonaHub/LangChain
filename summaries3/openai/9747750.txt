Sheehan's syndrome is a condition resulting from pituitary necrosis due to postpartum hemorrhage and hypovolemia, often leading to delayed diagnosis with symptoms resembling a sellar mass apoplexy. A case study presented a 32-year-old woman with postpartum pituitary apoplexy, where immediate imaging and pituitary function testing were conducted, leading to initial diagnostic challenges. The patient experienced severe headaches, visual impairment, and hormonal deficiencies, with differential diagnoses including pituitary adenoma infarction and lymphocytic hypophysitis.
