The text discusses the evolution of insulin delivery technology, starting with the groundbreaking use of insulin in 1922 to treat diabetes. It highlights the development of insulin syringes, insulin pens, needle-free injectors, and continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion pumps over the years. The focus is on advancements in technology and innovation that have shaped the development of more sophisticated automated devices for insulin delivery. The text also addresses the challenges and advancements in insulin delivery devices, such as the adoption of pens in the United States, the limitations of needle-free injectors, and the progress in continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion pumps. It emphasizes the importance of creating user-friendly devices that help patients comply with their therapy and manage their condition effectively, considering factors such as technical, clinical, user aspects, and health economics. The goal is to develop high-quality products that benefit users and improve the quality of life for individuals with diabetes.
