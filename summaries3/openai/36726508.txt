The text explores the intricate relationship between coaches and athletes in competitive sports, particularly in Chinese sports training teams. It delves into the impact of coaches' transformational leadership on athletes' well-being and basic psychological needs, highlighting the importance of meeting athletes' autonomy, competence, and relatedness needs. The study found that coaches' transformational leadership positively influences athletes' well-being and basic psychological needs, with female athletes showing higher basic psychological needs scores and well-being compared to male athletes. The text emphasizes the significance of coaches providing personalized care to athletes, fostering team cohesion, and promoting a supportive team atmosphere to enhance athletes' well-being. It also discusses the need for future research to explore the impact of coaches' leadership styles on athletes' well-being across different sports and cultural backgrounds.
