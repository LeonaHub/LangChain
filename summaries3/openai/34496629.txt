Policymakers are focusing on integrating care for individuals with both behavioral and physical health conditions, particularly those with serious mental illness, to improve access and outcomes. Various integrated care models have been implemented, combining mental health services with primary care in different settings. Four national initiatives (CCBHC, PBHCI, MHH, and PCMH) are examined, each targeting different populations and utilizing different financial strategies. These initiatives involve multidisciplinary teams providing services like chronic disease management and wellness programs. Key elements include population health management, self-management support, care management, and performance monitoring
