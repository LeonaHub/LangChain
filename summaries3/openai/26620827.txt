Posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is a significant mental health consequence of traumatic events, with a higher prevalence among women compared to men. Factors such as childhood maltreatment, sexual assault, and intimate partner violence are associated with an increased risk of developing PTSD in the general U.S. population. Female victims of intentional violence may experience higher rates of psychopathology and have poorer treatment outcomes compared to victims of unintended traumatic experiences. Research indicates that the likelihood of developing PTSD is greater among those who have directly experienced violence, with women experiencing IPV having a six-fold higher rate of PTSD compared to the general population.
