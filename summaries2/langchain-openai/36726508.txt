The text discusses the impact of coaches' transformational leadership on athletes' well-being in competitive sports. It highlights the importance of coaches in influencing athletes' psychological well-being and performance. The study explores how coaches' leadership styles can meet athletes' basic psychological needs, such as autonomy, competence, and belonging, to enhance their overall well-being. The research findings indicate that coaches' transformational leadership positively affects athletes' well-being, with basic psychological needs mediating this relationship. Gender also plays a moderating role, with female athletes showing higher basic psychological needs satisfaction and well-being compared to male athletes. The study suggests practical implications for coaches to enhance athletes' well-being through transformational leadership and individualized care. Limitations and future research directions are also discussed to further explore the relationship between coaches' leadership and athletes' well-being in different contexts.
