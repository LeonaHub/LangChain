The text discusses the history, impact, and prevention of rubella, focusing on congenital rubella syndrome (CRS) and the importance of vaccination. It describes a study conducted in the province of Florence to assess rubella seroprevalence in pediatric and adolescent populations. The study found a high seropositivity rate, indicating successful vaccination campaigns and herd immunity. The results emphasize the importance of maintaining high vaccination coverage to prevent CRS and eliminate rubella. The study also highlights the need to target unvaccinated individuals, especially adolescents, through effective communication strategies to further increase immunity levels.
