The text discusses a meta-analysis and systematic review conducted in Ethiopia to determine the prevalence and associated factors of Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) among survivors of trauma. The study included 13 articles with a total of 5874 participants, revealing a pooled prevalence of PTSD in Ethiopia at 39.28%. Factors associated with PTSD included poor social support, being female, having a previous mental illness, and witnessing traumatic events. The study highlighted the importance of social support in preventing PTSD and the need for targeted interventions for individuals at higher risk. The findings underscore the urgent need for interventions to address PTSD in Ethiopia, particularly among those exposed to traumatic events.
