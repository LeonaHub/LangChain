The text discusses the importance of the levelized cost of electricity (LCOE) in evaluating the market competitiveness of perovskite photovoltaic (PV) technology. It highlights the challenges in determining the LCOE for perovskite PVs, including uncertainties related to large-scale efficiency, stability, and manufacturing costs. The text explores various models and assumptions used in LCOE calculations for perovskite PVs, emphasizing the need for more precise evaluations. It also addresses the impact of system performance, stability, and manufacturing costs on the LCOE values. The text concludes by discussing future perspectives, such as refining LCOE models, improving stability, reducing manufacturing costs, and increasing production capacity to further reduce the LCOE of perovskite PV technology.
