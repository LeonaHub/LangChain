The text discusses myoepithelial lesions in head and neck pathology, focusing on myoepithelioma and myoepithelial carcinoma. Myoepithelioma is a rare benign salivary gland neoplasm composed of myoepithelial cells without a ductal component, while myoepithelial carcinoma is a malignant tumor composed of myoepithelial cells with invasive growth. The importance of a systematic approach to differential diagnosis using histology, immunohistochemistry, and potentially molecular studies is emphasized. Myoepithelioma comprises a small percentage of benign salivary gland tumors, more commonly found in major salivary glands, while myoepithelial carcinoma represents about 1% of malignant salivary gland neoplasms. The text also discusses benign salivary gland tumors like pleomorphic adenoma, oncocytoma, and intercalated duct lesions, as well as soft tissue neoplasms such as Schwannoma, Leiomyoma, and Extraskeletal Myxoid Chondrosarcoma. It highlights the histological and immunohistochemical characteristics of these tumors, emphasizing the importance of accurate diagnosis for appropriate treatment decisions.
