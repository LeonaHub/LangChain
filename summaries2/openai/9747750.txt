Sheehan's syndrome is a rare condition caused by pituitary necrosis following postpartum hemorrhage, leading to hypopituitarism. Diagnosis is often delayed, with symptoms mimicking those of a sellar mass apoplexy. Imaging studies like CT scans or MRIs may show a small sellar volume or an empty sella. A case study of a 32-year-old woman with postpartum pituitary apoplexy highlights the importance of immediate neuroradiological imaging and pituitary function testing. The text discusses the diagnostic challenges of differentiating Sheehan's syndrome from lymphocytic hypophysitis, emphasizing the significance of early diagnosis for optimal treatment.
