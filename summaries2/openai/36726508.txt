The text explores the relationship between coaches' transformational leadership, athletes' well-being, and basic psychological needs in competitive sports, focusing on Chinese sports culture. It discusses the impact of traditional paternalistic coaching styles on athletes and suggests that transformational leadership can enhance athletes' well-being and development. The study involved 432 athletes and found that coaches' transformational leadership positively predicted athletes' basic psychological needs and well-being, with gender playing a moderating role. Female athletes scored higher in basic psychological needs and well-being, indicating potential differences in how gender influences athletes' well-being. The study emphasizes the importance of coaches meeting athletes' basic psychological needs through transformational leadership to improve well-being and performance. Future research is recommended to validate these findings across different sports and cultural backgrounds.
