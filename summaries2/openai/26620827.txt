Posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is a significant mental health issue, especially common among women who have experienced childhood maltreatment, sexual assault, and intimate partner violence. Women facing intentional violence may have higher rates of psychopathology and poorer treatment outcomes. The prevalence of PTSD is notably higher among women who have encountered intimate partner violence compared to the general population, and it is linked to revictimization among abused women. Sexual assault can lead to long-term negative outcomes, including PTSD, depression, and anxiety. Racial and ethnic differences play a role in the impact of trauma and PTSD, with varying rates among different groups.
