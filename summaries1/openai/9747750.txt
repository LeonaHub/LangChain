Sheehan's syndrome is a rare condition caused by pituitary necrosis following postpartum hemorrhage and hypovolemia. Diagnosis can be challenging, often resembling other pituitary disorders like adenoma or hypophysitis. A case study of a 32-year-old woman with postpartum pituitary apoplexy highlighted the importance of considering the clinical context and using imaging studies to confirm the diagnosis. Differentiating Sheehan's syndrome from other conditions is crucial due to varying endocrine features and imaging findings. Another case study discussed a patient with Sheehan syndrome who underwent MRI imaging, showing a mass under the optic chiasm that spontaneously shrank.
