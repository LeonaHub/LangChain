The bone marrow contains hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) and mesenchymal stromal cells (MSCs) that are crucial for hematopoiesis and tissue maintenance. HSCs can differentiate into various blood cell lineages, while MSCs can become adipocytes or osteoblasts. The bone marrow niche influences cell fate decisions and energy demands are high due to blood cell production, requiring ATP from glycolysis or oxidative phosphorylation. Hypoxia in the niche affects cell behavior, with HIF1a and VEGF playing roles in differentiation and vascular development. Various substrates are involved in ATP generation and differentiation, with AMPK activation influencing these processes.
