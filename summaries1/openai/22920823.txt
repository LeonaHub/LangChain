The text discusses the evolution of insulin delivery devices, from the first use of insulin in 1921 to the development of modern devices like injector pens and continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion (CSII) pumps. It highlights the challenges and advancements in insulin delivery technology, including the emergence of needle-free injectors, disposable pens, and wearable devices like patch pumps. The importance of patient engagement and compliance in diabetes management is emphasized, with a focus on creating user-friendly, effective insulin delivery systems. The text also touches on the future of diabetes management, predicting increased use of patch pumps, mobile applications, and continuous glucose monitoring. It stresses the need for technology to empower patients and simplify diabetes management, rather than adding complexity. The development of an ideal disposable pen for diabetes management is discussed, emphasizing the importance of considering health economics, technical aspects, and user needs in creating a beneficial product.
