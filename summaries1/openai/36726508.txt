The text discusses the impact of coaches' transformational leadership on athletes' well-being in competitive sports, emphasizing the importance of coaches meeting athletes' basic psychological needs to enhance their performance and emotional well-being. The study conducted surveys with 432 athletes, finding positive correlations between coaches' transformational leadership, athletes' basic psychological needs, and well-being. Gender differences were identified, with female athletes showing higher basic psychological needs satisfaction and well-being. The study used statistical analyses to explore these relationships, highlighting the significance of coaches' leadership styles in influencing athletes' well-being and team cohesion. The text suggests that coaches should act as role models, provide personalized care, and foster autonomy and belonging within the team to improve athletes' well-being. The study acknowledges limitations and suggests future research directions, concluding that perceived transformational leadership positively impacts athletes' well-being, with gender moderating this relationship.
