The text emphasizes the significance of surface texture in manufacturing processes and its impact on functional properties of machined elements. It discusses errors and uncertainties in surface texture measurement, including sources of errors like stylus flight and temperature issues. The study analyzes the effects of errors on surface texture parameters, focusing on additional valleys and repetitive measurements using optical methods. It describes the measurement and analysis of surface textures, highlighting the impact of different processing operations on surface textures. The study examines changes in various parameters due to the presence of circular valleys and repetitive measurements, noting the stability and variability of different parameters on various surfaces. It concludes that different parameters react differently to measurement errors and surface types, suggesting the use of specific parameters for improved surface texture assessment in industrial applications. The text also mentions the need for future research to explore the effects of other types of measurement errors.
