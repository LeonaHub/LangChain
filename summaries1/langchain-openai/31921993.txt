The text discusses the development and implementation of a film-based curriculum aimed at addressing mistreatment in the medical student learning environment from the perspectives of faculty, residents, nurses, and staff. The curriculum includes film screenings, discussions, and teaching points for both students and educators. Evaluation data shows that the film resonated with viewers, leading to increased understanding and potential behavior change. The curriculum facilitated intergenerational dialogue and enhanced empathy among participants. While the curriculum has been positively received, further research is needed to assess its long-term impact. The text emphasizes the importance of promoting a positive learning environment through shared responsibility and cross-generational dialogue.
