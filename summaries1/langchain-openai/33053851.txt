The text discusses the history, impact, and prevention of rubella, focusing on congenital rubella syndrome (CRS) and the importance of vaccination. It highlights the global efforts to eliminate rubella and CRS through vaccination campaigns and surveillance. The study conducted in the province of Florence assessed rubella seroprevalence in pediatric and adolescent subjects, showing a high immunity level due to successful vaccination programs. The results indicate a significant decrease in susceptible individuals compared to previous years, emphasizing the importance of maintaining high vaccination coverage to prevent CRS and ensure herd immunity. Despite progress, continued monitoring and adherence to vaccination guidelines are crucial to eliminate rubella and CRS.
