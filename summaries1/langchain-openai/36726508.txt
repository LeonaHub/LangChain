The text discusses the relationship between coaches' transformational leadership, athletes' basic psychological needs, and athletes' well-being in the context of competitive sports. It highlights the importance of coaches' leadership styles in influencing athletes' well-being and how meeting athletes' basic psychological needs can enhance their overall satisfaction and performance. The study explores the mediating role of basic psychological needs and the moderating role of gender in this relationship. The results indicate that coaches' transformational leadership positively impacts athletes' well-being, with basic psychological needs mediating this relationship. Additionally, gender moderates the relationship between coaches' leadership and athletes' well-being, with female athletes showing higher improvements in well-being when their basic psychological needs are met. The study provides practical implications for coaches to enhance athletes' well-being through transformational leadership and individualized care. However, the study acknowledges limitations in terms of sample demographics and suggests future research directions to validate the findings in different contexts.
